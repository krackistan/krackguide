  • Labour
    • Hallways
      • Blown Shift = $39.90
      • Pre-vacuum pick-up
        • Before you start vacuuming, make sure the floor is clear of anything bigger than a quarter.
      • Vacuum Acquisition
        • The vacuums are generally found in front of the maintenance room. The maintenance room is on the 10's floor, directly left at the bottom of the stairs.
        • The vacuums are backpacks with long hoses connected to silver pipes for extension.
        • If they are not here, then check to see if someone else is using it before going to the maintenance manager.
      • Vacuum Maintenance
        • The extension cord plugs into the backpack near the waist belt and also near the power switch.
        • Before using it, make sure the bag is empty. To do this unscrew the top of the canister and remove the cylindrical bag and filter. Empty the bag into the trash and gently remove the dust from the filter.
        • Replace the bag and make sure the intake port is free of debris.
        • If, while vacuuming it does not seem to be working, make sure the intake is not clogged. If it is not clogged and the problem persists, you may need to remove the canister lid and make sure there is nothing in the black tubing by clearing it with a broom handle or the like.
    • Bathrooms
      • Blown Shift = $66.50
      • IMPORTANT
        • There's a major difference between cleaning and sanitizing. Both should be done to all surfaces during a bathroom shift.
        • Make sure the bathroom is well ventilated (door/window open) while using bleach and other cleaning chemicals.
        • Use 1/4 cup of bleach for 1 gallon of cold water (1 tablespoon for 1 quart)
        • Yellow sponges are specifically for the toilets.
        • Blue sponges are used for everything else.
      • Sinks
        1. Using the all-purpose cleaner and blue sponge, (Green Works spray bottle) scrub the counter-top/sink bowls/faucet handles and nozzles to remove dirt, grease and debris.
        2. Rinse well with water.
        3. Using bleach solution (sanitizer spray bottle) spray the surfaces to disinfect.
        4. Allow solution to dry for at least 2 minutes.
        • **Bleach is NEVER effective if the surface is not clean prior to application**
      • Mirrors
        1. Remove any out-dated fliers.
        2. Use the all-purpose cleaner (Green Works spray bottle) to scrub off any filth on the mirrors.
        3. Dry mirrors by using crumpled newspaper as a squeegee.
        • Toilets
          1. Flush toilet.
          2. Sprinkle Comet powder or spray bleach solution into the bowl, making sure to apply under the toilet bowl lip.
          3. Let cleaner soak in the bowl while you clean the outside with the all-purpose cleaner (Green Works spray bottle) and sponge. Be sure to disinfect toilet handle.
          4. When the outside is clean and sanitized, use the toilet brush to thoroughly scrub the bowl.
          5. Flush toilet multiple times to rinse both the bowl and brush.
          • Special attention should be given to mineral build-ups along the water level, the back of the bowl and under the lip.
        • Showers
          1. Remove any toiletries.
          2. Spray the bleach solution on shower walls and floor.
          3. Scrub shower, focussing on areas with more mildew and mold. The grout between the tiles is the most important place to scrub. Use a toothbrush if necessary.
          4. Rinse the bleach solution off using cold water from the shower-head. If there are areas the water flow doesn't reach, be sure to rinse with a wet cloth or sponge.
        • Vents
          1. Using gloves and standing on a chair, remove built-up dust from vents on ceiling.
          2. If collection is particularly clogged remove entire vent and spray down with hose in courtyard. (or notify the maintenance manager)
        • Remove Trash and Compost
          1. Remove old fliers and clean up any empty shampoo bottles.
          2. Separate recyclables (if applicable) and put them in the recycling area.
          3. Get new trash bags from under the milk dispensers in the outer kitchen.
          4. Take trash and compost bins to the dumpsters on the CK dock.
          • Take the elevator to the ' L ' level and exit left.
          • Prop open the first door (generally a brick close by) so you may return via the elevator.
          • Follow to the right, down the corridor to the CK dock.
          • On the far wall, there are two dumpsters. One for compost on the right (green) and the other for trash on the left (brown).
        • Restock
          1. Make sure the bathroom is completely stocked with toilet paper, paper towels and soap.
          • Toilet paper and paper towels can be found in the laundry room in the 200's bathroom. Supplies should be in the wardrobe and if not notify the kitchen manager (responsible for these purchases).
          • Soap packets can be found in the cupboards below the silverware in the outer kitchen.
        • Sweep and Mop
          1. Sweep bathroom. If no broom in bathroom, there generally is a collection of dust bins and brooms in the common room next to the workshift board.
          2. Place a "cleaning in process" sign on the door to ensure nobody walks on the drying floor while mopping.
          3. Mop bathroom. Mops can be found in the mop closest located in the outer kitchen in between the ridge airlock exit and the edge of the countertop.
          • Take a clean mop and bucket to the bathroom. If no clean mops, use the mop sink inside the closet to ring out the dirt from the mop head.
          • Fill up the bucket in the shower and put 1/4th cup of bleach to ~ 1 gallon of cold water.
          • Submerge the mop into the bucket and allow for full absorption of the cleaning solution.
          • Wring out the mop-head by placing it in the top compartment of the bucket and pulling the handle down enough to make the mop-head damp, not soaking.
          • Start on one side of the bathroom and apply pressure into the ground to remove dirt. Continue in one location until you have removed dirt.
          • Wring out mop-head as it gets dirtier.
          • Refill the bucket as the solution gets dirtier (~half way across floor) by emptying it slowly in the shower. Refill and continue.
          • Allow floor to dry. Clean mop-head and hang it up to dry in mop closet.

        • Online
          • **To verify that someone did their workshift, you must have seen this person do the workshift or seen the results of the completed workshift**
          1. Visit http://workshift.bsc.coop/caz/, the main workshift page.
          2. Under "Workshift Links" choose "Sign off of a Shift Online"
          3. Choose the name of the person you are signing off in the drop down menu after "Workshifter"
          4. Choose your name in the drop down menu after "Verifier" and choose which of their workshifts you are verifying.
          5. If this shift is not shown on the page, either they have missed the time allowed for verification or this is a speciality shift in which case they should have a manager sign them off.
          6. If there shift is shown, choose it and submit by clicking "sign off"
        • Written
          • **To verify that someone did their workshift, you must have seen this person do the workshift or seen the results of the completed workshift**
          1. If the workshift manager has chosen to use written sign-offs or the online system is faulty, there will be a daily list of assigned workshifts on a board in the common room near the freezer.
          2. To verify someone's shift you will sign your name next to their specific workshift that you are verifying.
          3. The sheet will be collected daily by the workshift manager, meaning you must be verified the day that you did the workshift.
